## Open decisions

1. **Logo bar above customer story?** **In.** Populate with 1 real (DealHub) + 5 plausible enterprise B2B marks.

## Audience filter

If a senior PMM director would skim past it in 3 seconds without losing the pitch, it stays.
If a paragraph reads more useful to a VC than to a PMM, it leaves.

---

## Marketing numbers

Marketing copy doesn't have to be precise. Use plausible, outcome-shaped numbers across the page; replace with verified data once the customer has signed off.

- **Logo bar:** 1 real (DealHub) + 5 plausible enterprise B2B wordmarks.
- **Customer story metrics** (outcomes, not engagement):
  - *60%* less time on competitor research
  - *3×* faster launch artifact production
  - *Days → Hours* battle card refresh time
- **Closing CTA tagline:** *"First battle card live in <24 hours."*
- **Pain section stat:** *"~60% of a PMM's week"*. Keep.

---

## Capability bullets (per tab)

Three feature-named bullets per tab. Specific verbs and nouns; nothing generic.

- **Buyer Intel:** Persona generator from Gong transcripts · ICP scorecards by segment · Objection ledger linked to deals
- **Competitor Intel:** Pricing-page diff detection · Feature-launch tracker · Competitor messaging timeline
- **Positioning:** Buyer-language value props · Differentiation matrix · Messaging that updates with the market
- **Sales Enablement:** Auto-updating battle cards · Per-segment objection playbooks · Win/loss intelligence linked to deals
- **Launches:** Launch timeline builder · Artifact bundle (deck, brief, one-pager) · Cross-functional coordination
