# Oden Positioning and Messaging

Status: Draft derived from the current homepage

## One-line summary

Oden helps product marketing teams stop rebuilding context from scratch by turning buyer signals, competitor changes, and internal knowledge into current messaging, launches, and sales assets.

## Who this is for

### Primary ICP

Growth-stage B2B software companies with a real product marketing function.

Common traits:
- PMM owns positioning, launches, competitive intel, and sales enablement
- buyer knowledge lives across calls, docs, Slack, and tools
- the team is expected to move fast without losing message quality
- sales, marketing, and product all depend on PMM outputs staying current

Typical buyers:
- Director / Head of Product Marketing
- VP of Marketing with PMM under them
- Founding or first PMM at a scaling B2B company

### Secondary ICP

Adjacent stakeholders who benefit from Oden but are not the first buyer:
- sales enablement leaders
- revenue leaders who rely on current battle cards and objection handling
- founders and CEOs at earlier-stage companies where PMM context is still fragmented

## Core problem

Product marketing runs on context, but that context does not live anywhere durable.

Today, PMMs repeatedly have to:
- re-read sales calls to recover buyer language
- re-check competitor sites and pricing pages for changes
- dig through docs, Slack, and notes to rebuild the latest story
- update downstream assets manually across launches, battle cards, and content

The result is slow PMM throughput, stale outputs, and constant rework.

## Product category

Recommended category framing:

Oden is a product marketing system built around shared context.

Plain-English explanation:
- it is not just a writing tool
- it is not just competitive intel
- it is not just battle cards
- it is a shared context layer plus the PMM workflows that run on top of it

## Positioning statement

For product marketing teams at growth-stage B2B companies, Oden is a product marketing system that keeps messaging, launches, and sales assets current by turning calls, competitor moves, and internal docs into one shared context.

Unlike generic AI tools or disconnected point solutions, Oden compounds with every new input and keeps PMM work aligned across workflows.

## Objections and response

### "Is this just ChatGPT for PMM?"

Response:
No. Generic AI starts from the prompt. Oden starts from your accumulated buyer, competitor, and product context, then applies that across PMM workflows.

### "Is this really broader than sales enablement?"

Response:
Yes. Sales enablement is one workflow. The broader value is that the same context powers positioning, launches, buyer intel, content, and competitor intel too.

### "Why not keep using Gong, Notion, Docs, and Claude?"

Response:
Those tools store pieces of the work, but they do not keep the PMM story current across workflows. Oden turns those scattered inputs into one system.

### "What is the real moat?"

Response:
The moat is shared context that compounds over time and stays usable across multiple PMM workflows, not just one-off generation.

## Recommended messaging hierarchy

If we want the cleanest story, the order should be:

1. Product marketing compounds
2. The reason is one shared context
3. That context powers six PMM workflows
4. The outputs stay current across the team
5. The proof is customer outcomes and time saved
